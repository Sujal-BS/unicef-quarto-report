Vitamin A Supplementation: Saving Children's Lives Worldwide

Sujal Boodgere Sunil - A00096721 | UNICEF Data Analyst Report

Why This Matters

Vitamin A deficiency remains one of the most serious public health problems affecting children in low- and middle-income countries. It is a leading cause of preventable blindness and significantly increases the risk of death from common childhood infections such as measles and diarrhoea.

According to global health data, Vitamin A supplementation can reduce child mortality by up to 23% among children aged 6–59 months in high-risk populations.

Key Insights from the Analysis

  • Global coverage has improved since 2000, but progress is uneven across regions.
  • Higher GDP per capita is strongly associated with better supplementation coverage.
  • Several countries still report extremely low coverage (<10%), putting millions of children at risk.
  • The COVID-19 pandemic caused temporary disruptions in many national programmes.

Call to Action

UNICEF and partner governments must prioritise sustained funding, stronger supply chains, and community awareness campaigns in low-coverage countries. Every additional percentage point in coverage has the potential to save thousands of young lives annually.
