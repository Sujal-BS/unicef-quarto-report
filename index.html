Vitamin A Supplementation: Saving Children's Lives Worldwide

Sujal Boodgere Sunil - A00096721 | UNICEF Data Analyst Report

Executive Summary

Vitamin A deficiency is a major preventable cause of childhood blindness and mortality, particularly in developing countries. Routine Vitamin A supplementation for children aged 6–59 months has proven to reduce under-five mortality by up to 23%.

This report analyses global coverage trends using UNICEF data, examines its relationship with economic indicators and child mortality (ages 5–9), and highlights areas where urgent action is needed.

Key Findings

  • While global average coverage has improved since 2000, large disparities persist between countries and regions.
  • Higher GDP per capita shows a strong positive correlation with better Vitamin A supplementation coverage.
  • Several countries still have coverage rates below 10%, leaving millions of children vulnerable.
  • The COVID-19 pandemic caused significant disruptions to supplementation campaigns in 2020.
  • Countries with low coverage often experience higher child mortality rates in the 5–9 age group.

Recommendations & Call to Action

UNICEF, governments, and international partners should focus on:

  • Improving logistics and supply chain reliability in low-coverage countries.
  • Integrating Vitamin A supplementation with routine immunisation and maternal health programmes.
  • Enhancing community awareness and monitoring systems to ensure consistent delivery.
  • Increasing targeted funding for countries with coverage below 50%.

Every percentage point increase in coverage can save thousands of young lives each year.
