Vitamin A Supplementation: Saving Children's Lives Worldwide

Sujal Boodgere Sunil - A00096721 | BAA1030 UNICEF Data Analyst Report

1. Executive Summary

Vitamin A deficiency is one of the leading preventable causes of childhood blindness and mortality. Simple twice-yearly supplementation can reduce child mortality by up to 23% in children aged 6–59 months.

This report analyses global coverage using UNICEF data and highlights where urgent action is needed.

2. Why This Issue Matters

Every year, hundreds of thousands of children suffer or die due to Vitamin A deficiency. Supplementation is a highly cost-effective way to protect them. Understanding current coverage helps UNICEF and governments take better action.

3. Key Findings

  • Global coverage has improved since 2000, but large regional gaps remain.
  • Higher GDP per capita strongly correlates with better supplementation rates.
  • Several countries still have coverage below 10%.
  • Coverage dropped noticeably in 2020 due to COVID-19.
  • Low coverage is often linked to higher child mortality (ages 5–9).

4. Data & Methodology

Data from UNICEF Indicator 1 (Vitamin A coverage) and Indicator 2 (child mortality) were merged with World Bank metadata using Polars. All visualisations were created with plotnine in Python.

6. Recommendations & Call to Action

To improve child survival, we recommend:

  • Strengthening supply chains for reliable delivery.
  • Integrating supplementation with routine immunisation.
  • Running community awareness campaigns.
  • Providing extra support to low-coverage countries.

Every percentage point increase in coverage can save thousands of young lives.
